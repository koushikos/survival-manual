# Complete Long-Term Survival Manual: Rebuilding Civilization After Global Collapse

**Authors:** Multidisciplinary Team (Survival Engineer, Civil Engineer, Agricultural Scientist, Medical Doctor, Renewable Energy Engineer, Security Strategist, Off-Grid Infrastructure Designer, Anthropologist)

**Version:** 1.0  
**Purpose:** Step-by-step technical handbook for 10-20 survivors to establish sustainable life for 80+ years using natural resources and simple technology.  
**Assumptions:** No modern infrastructure, electricity, internet, hospitals, supply chains, or governments.  
**Target Audience:** Beginners with basic tools (axe, shovel, saw, knife, rope, nails if available).

## SECTION 1 — LOCATION SELECTION AND SETTLEMENT PLANNING

### Ideal Location Criteria
- **Climate Zones:** Temperate zones (USDA zones 6-9) with 4 seasons: mild winters (< -10°C), warm summers (< 35°C), 800-1500mm annual rainfall. Avoid extremes: tropics (diseases), deserts (water scarcity), poles (cold).
- **Distance from Cities:** >200km from major cities (>100k pop) to avoid refugees/looting. >50km from highways.
- **Freshwater:** Permanent stream/spring or groundwater <10m deep. Daily need: 4L/person + 10L/agriculture/livestock.
- **Soil:** Loamy soil (sand+silt+clay). Fertile: dark, crumbly, earthworm-rich.
- **Natural Disasters:** Avoid floodplains, fault lines, hurricane coasts, volcanoes.
- **Defenses:** Valleys with hills/mountains for barriers, dense forests for concealment, single access road/trail.

### Step-by-Step Land Survey (No Modern Tools)
1. **Walk the Grid:** Divide area into 100m x 100m squares using pacing (1 pace ≈ 0.75m). Mark corners with rocks/sticks.
2. **Terrain Mapping:** 
   - Climb high point. Sketch horizon on bark/paper with charcoal.
   - Note slopes (>30° too steep), valleys (good for wind protection).
   - Diagram: [High hill] → [Valley floor: housing] → [Stream] → [Uphill: fields].
3. **Soil Evaluation:**
   | Test | Method | Good Result |
   |------|--------|-------------|
   | Texture | Squeeze handful: forms ball, crumbles easily | Loam (holds shape briefly) |
   | pH | Mix soil + rainwater in jar, add vinegar (fizz=alkaline), baking soda (fizz=acidic) | Neutral (no fizz) |
   | Fertility | Dig pit, count earthworms/10cm²; smell (earthy, not sour) | >5 worms, earthy |
4. **Groundwater Location:**
   - Willow trees or damp soil indicate water.
   - Dig test pits (2m deep) in low areas at dawn; if sides dampen, groundwater present.
5. **Water Testing:** Let settle 24h, sniff/taste. Boil sample; if clear/no odor, good.

### Settlement Layout for 10-20 People (5-10 Acres)
```
Defensive Perimeter (thorny hedge + ditch)
↑
Watchtower --- Housing (10-15 cabins, 4x4m each) --- Central Fire/Meeting
 |                                       |
Livestock (goats/chickens)         Storage (grain/root cellar)
 |
Water System (tanks/well)
 ↓
Farming (1-2 acres crops) --- Energy (solar/wind hill) --- Orchards
```
- **Housing:** Central cluster for defense.
- **Farming:** South-facing slope.
- **Water:** Elevated tanks for gravity flow.
- **Perimeter:** 2m deep ditch + thorny bushes (blackberry/hawthorn).

## SECTION 2 — WATER SYSTEM (LONG-TERM WATER SECURITY)

**Daily Requirement:** 4L/person drinking/cooking + 20L/hygiene + 50L/agriculture/livestock = 1,000L/group/day.

### Water Sources
1. **Rainwater Harvesting (Primary):**
   - Roof catchment: 100m² roof yields 100L/mm rain.
   - **Build Gutter System:**
     1. Split logs lengthwise for gutters (1m sections).
     2. Nail/hang under eaves, slope 1cm/m to downspout (bamboo/PVC).
     3. First-flush diverter: 20L barrel diverts initial dirty water.
   - **Storage Tanks:** 10,000L total (10x 1m³ barrels).
     1. Dig pit, line with clay/sand (seal with lime plaster: burnt shells + sand).
     2. Clay jars or log hollows as backups.

2. **Manual Well (Secondary):**
   - **Digging (4 people, 1 week for 10m well):**
     1. Mark 1.5m diameter circle. Dig in 30cm layers.
     2. Shore walls: Logs horizontal, cross-braced (remove post-water hit).
     3. Bucket brigade: Rope + pulley from tree.
     4. Stop at water table; line bottom with gravel.
   - **Hand Pump:** Rope pump – PVC pipe + rope with discs.

3. **Springs/Rivers:** Divert with canal; screen debris.

### Purification Methods (All Water Boiled First)
1. **Sand/Charcoal Filter:**
   ```
   Top: Gravel (5cm)
   Sand (20cm, fine/wash)
   Charcoal (10cm, burnt hardwood)
   Gravel (5cm)
   → Clean container
   ```
   Build: 1m tall barrel/clay pot with holes.

2. **Slow Sand Biofilter:** 1m sand + gravel + biofilm (weeks to mature). 100L/day clean.

3. **Solar Distillation:** Black basin + glass/plastic cover → condense drip. 5L/day/sun.

**Storage:** Dark, covered, elevated. Rotate every 3 days. Drought: Ration 2L/person/day + dig snow/plant transpiration.

## SECTION 3 — FOOD PRODUCTION SYSTEM

**Calorie Goal:** 2,500/person/day = 50M calories/year for 20 people. 70% plants, 30% animals.

### Soil Preparation
- **Compost:** 1:1 green (grass) + brown (leaves). Pile 1m³, turn weekly (hot=60°C kills pathogens).
- **Fertilizer:** Urine (dilute 10:1), manure (aged 6mo), wood ash (potassium).
- **Crop Rotation:** Legumes → grains → roots → rest.

### Survival Crops (Plant 1 Acre)
| Crop | Yield (tons/acre) | Plant/Harvest | Calories/kg |
|------|-------------------|---------------|-------------|
| Potatoes | 20 | Spring/Fall | 800 |
| Corn | 5 | Spring/Fall | 365 |
| Beans | 1 | Summer | 350 |
| Wheat | 2 | Fall/Spring | 340 |

**Planting:** Double-dig beds (30cm deep), hills for potatoes (hilling prevents green toxins).

**Irrigation:** Drip – pierced bamboo pipes from water tank.

**Pests:** Companion planting (marigolds repel nematodes), hand-pick, neem oil.

### Perennials (Year 1 Plant, Harvest Year 3+)
- Apples/pears/nuts: 10 trees each. Grafted scions in moist soil.
- Berries: Blackberries along perimeter.

### Animals
- **Chickens (50):** Coop (scratch floor, nests). Feed scraps/grains/insects.
- **Goats (10):** Milk 2L/day. Fodder: Browse trees.
- **Fish Pond:** Dig 20x20m, stock from river (tilapia).

### Preservation
1. **Drying:** Slice thin, sun/rack 2-3 days.
2. **Root Cellar:** 3m deep pit, straw-insulated.
3. **Ferment:** Sauerkraut (salt cabbage 2%).

**Grain Storage:** Airtight clay silos elevated on rocks.

## SECTION 4 — SHELTER AND INFRASTRUCTURE

### Log Cabin (4x4m, 2 People, 2 Weeks)
1. **Clearing:** Fell trees (notch base, axe topple), limb/debark.
2. **Foundation:** Stone piers (frost line deep).
3. **Walls:** Logs notched saddle/corners. Chink with clay/moss.
4. **Roof:** Poles + thatch (grass bundles, 30cm thick).
5. **Insulation:** Mud plaster inside/out.

**Composting Toilet:** 1m pit, seat box, ash cover (kills pathogens).
